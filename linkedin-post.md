# ApplyTrail - LinkedIn Post

---

I've been in this job search myself, which is exactly why this exists.

The current job market is noisy, slow, and unpredictable. Gmail wasn't really designed for the volume of job search communication people are dealing with right now. It made sense for the world we had before, but today's market creates a level of inbox traffic that's hard for anyone to manage.

Application updates land in the same "Updates" tab as promo codes and newsletters. Interview invites get buried. Rejections disappear. And across dozens of portals and threads, it becomes surprisingly hard to answer a basic question: Where do all my applications actually stand?

It's not a personal organization problem. It's a tooling gap.

So I built something to make this part easier.

**ApplyTrail** is a free, open-source tool that connects to Gmail and automatically tracks your job search. Every application, interview invite, offer, and rejection is organized into a clean dashboard so you can finally see the full picture. It also nudges you when an application has gone quiet for two weeks, so you know exactly who to follow up with and when.

A few things I cared about while designing it:

→ Privacy first. Everything stays on your machine. No cloud, no server.
→ Read-only access. It can't send, delete, or modify anything in your inbox.
→ Free and accessible. No subscription. Optional AI classification using your own Claude API key, but it works without one.
→ Open source. Take it, fork it, improve it.
